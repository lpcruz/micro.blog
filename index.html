Striking a Balance: Simplifying Choices for Better DevEx

May 10, 2024

Software is fleeting and continuously changing so making decisions about tools can be quite the challenge. Ultimately, engineers will tend to have a lot of opinions. This week, my colleague dropped a truth-bomb:

“We always underestimate the maintenance required for variations.”

There was a recent work discussion on node test frameworks. We debated the merits of options like the native Node runner, Jest, and Vitest, realizing that too many choices can overwhelm rather than empower developers.

So, how do we strike a balance? Simplify. Whether it’s through a tech radar or a playbook, recommending the most widely used tool or the latest “hotness” streamlines decision-making without drowning developers in options. Because in the end, being prescriptive isn’t about stifling creativity ‚Äì it’s about guiding developers through the maze with clarity.

Goal: 200 New Miles in 2024?

Apr 27, 2024

Late in 2023, I found a cool app called wandrer.earth that motivates you to explore unfamiliar places and veer off from your typical routes. It effectively tracks new miles traveled and integrates seamlessly with Strava, where I record my runs. After integrating it with Strava, it automatically runs a backfill of your recorded runs to tally the total unique miles. As of the date of this blog post, I have achieved the following:

  • Covered 503.9 new miles running
  • Ran in 4 countries: United States, Canada, Dominican Republic, and the Philippines
  • Ran in 21 different provinces/states

In 2024, I’ve committed to run a new route whenever I travel and so far I’ve ran 54.99 new miles.

My goal is to his 200 unique miles by the end of 2024 (+263%). Sounds…reasonable?
